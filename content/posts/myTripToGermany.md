I spent my entire summer in Germany, as an intern at Forschungszentrum Jülich through the DAAD RISE Program. It was a great experience all around, and I highly recommend anyone interested in spending some time abroad during his/her undergrad to consider this program, particularly over a typical *study* abroad. 

- Unlike a study abroad where you are paying tuition to attend, internships abroad are more often paid positions.
- In my personal opinion, spending time abroad during the summer is better than during the academic year, since you'll be missing out on less back home.

There were also things specific to my experience that made my time in Germany very worthwhile. I met wonderful peers, many in my same program, and mentors, whom I worked under. The lab I worked in was quite young, and mostly operated by Ph.D. students, all of whom were friendly and happy to teach me things. And indeed, I learned a lot. I'm very fortunate that I was entrusted with quite an independent research project -- a first for me -- and it gave me a great, authentic sense for what working in a wet lab, in a research group, and in a national laboratory are like.

For me, one of my favorite things about Germany was all of the biking I got to do. Especially for being in a relatively quiet part of Germany, the quality and density of biking paths in the area were truly astounding. My favorite bike ride has to be my journey to Aachen: about 30 km in ~3 hours (maybe 2 if you don't count the time I spent lost or detouring). 
Also nearly every weekend I got to travel some place new in Europe. France, Luxembourg, Belgium, the Netherlands; it was spectacular. I'm very thankful they all were very smooth trips, despite this being my first time to travel internationally without my parents. I learned a great deal about travel planning and gained a great deal of confidence in doing so on my own.

I'll try and update this post later with some of my favorite photos another time!!!
