## Hi! My name is Hugo.

I'm a third year undergraduate at UCLA, studying Materials Science and Engineering (with a focus on Electronic Materials). I’m also pursuing a technical breadth in Math/Computer Science. I have great intrest for materials research and am hoping to combine it with my growing skill set in software to solve complex problems. I’m honored to have received the Chin Family Endowed Engineering Scholarship and Knapp Scholarship for Ceramics Studies, which support my academic journey.

I am an undergraduate assistant in the [Ab Initio Simulations Lab at UCLA](https://www.amartyabanerjee.com/), under the guidance of Professor Amartya Banerjee. During my first year in the lab, I was an IBM Research Scholar through the Semiconductor Research Corporation's Undergraduate Research Program. It's now the start of my second year in the lab, and I'm writing in-house scripts to add band structure visualization and custom k-point path diagonalization to MSPARC, a MATLAB real space density functional theory package. Last summer, I was funded by the DAAD (German Academic Exchange Service) RISE Program to study the long-term stability of neural implants at Forschungszentrum Jülich in Germany, one of Europe's largest research centers.

I also have some work experience! I was UCLA's D1 Men's and Women's Volleyball Teams' Student DJ for all home games last season, and when I'm back in my hometown for breaks, I occasionally work at my local Cedar Hill Cemetery.

My hobbies include making music, playing jazz piano, and staying active -- weightlifting, basketball, running, and more. Currently looking for ways to learn more about improv and comedy.
