## Hi! My name is Hugo.

I'm a third year undergraduate at UCLA, studying Materials Science and Engineering. I was born and raised in New York but, unfortunately, never seemed to get an accent to match.

I'm currently a Materials Engineering student at UCLA, where I’m also pursuing a technical breadth in Math/Computer Science. I have great intrest for materials research and am hoping to combine it with my growing skill set in software to solve complex problems. I’m honored to have received the Chin Family Endowed Engineering Scholarship and Knapp Scholarship for Ceramics Studies, which support my academic journey.

Under the guidance of Professor Amartya Banerjee, I am an undergraduate assistant in the [Ab Initio Simulations Lab at UCLA](https://www.amartyabanerjee.com/). During my first year in the lab, I was an IBM Research Scholar through the Semiconductor Research Corporation's Undergraduate Research Program. It's now the start of my second year in the lab, and I'm writing in-house scripts to add band structure visualization and custom k-point path diagonalization to MSPARC, a MATLAB real space density functional theory package. Las summer, I was funded by the DAAD (German Academic Exchange Service) RISE Program to study the long-term stability of neural implants at Forschungszentrum Jülich in Germany, one of Europe's largest research centers.

I also have some work experience! I was UCLA's D1 Men's and Women's Volleyball Teams' Student DJ for all home games last season. When I'm back in my hometown for breaks, I occasionally work at my local Cedar Hill Cemetery.

My hobbies include making music, playing jazz piano, and staying active -- weightlifting, basketball, running, and more.
